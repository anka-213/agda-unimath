Idea

Unlabeled partitions, also known as Ferrers diagrams, of a type A record the number of ways in which A can be decomposed as the dependent pair type of a family of inhabited types. More precisely, a Ferrers diagram of a type A consists of a type X and a family Y of inhabited types over X such that Î£ X Y is merely equivalent to A. A finite Finite ferrers diagram of a finite type A consists of a finite type X and a family Y of inhabited finite types over X such that Î£ X Y is merely equivalent to A. The number of finite Ferrers diagrams of A is the partition number of the cardinality of A.
